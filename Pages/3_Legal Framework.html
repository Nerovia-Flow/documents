🛡️ Core clauses – All contracts
✅ Hardware ownership
All sensors, gateway, battery, and enclosure remain the exclusive property of NeroviaFlow. The client has a right of use during the contract term. No transfer of ownership.
💥 Damage & loss liability
The client is fully liable for physical damage, theft, or loss caused by negligence, unauthorised modification, or failure to follow basic care instructions. Replacement cost: current market price (approx. $450‑$500). Normal wear and tear is not chargeable.
⏱️ Service level agreement (SLA)
99.5% data transmission uptime (excl. scheduled maintenance). Alert response within 2 hours (24/7). Defective hardware replaced at no cost (battery consumable: $15 after 2 years, free first replacement for Premium).
📊 Data ownership & monetisation
Client owns raw operational data. NeroviaFlow may use anonymised, aggregated data for benchmarking, research, and resale to utilities and researchers ($30/site/month from Y4). Client consent obtained in the contract.
💤 Sleeping partner – Non‑equity profit‑share agreement
📜 Legal formalisation – Silent partner (non‑shareholder)
In accordance with the legal expert's advice, the terms agreed with the sleeping partner are as follows:

• Cash contribution: $25k (paid in Y1).
• Nature of investment: participatory advance (or "loan with profit‑sharing rights"), without equity participation in the company. The sleeping partner receives no shares and has no voting rights.
• Compensation: 50% of net profits (excl. VAT) until a cumulative repayment of $50k is reached. Beyond this threshold, the profit‑sharing right ends permanently.
• Duration: repayment must occur no later than Y3 (in line with projections).
• No rights to assets or capital gains: the sleeping partner has no claim on the company's assets (including software, goodwill, or any future exit proceeds).
• Formalisation: a specific "silent partner agreement" will be drafted, separate from the company's bylaws, incorporating these terms. This agreement grants no governance rights or veto power.
• Objective: to secure the transaction while preserving full ownership and control of the business by the founders.

⚖️ Limitation of liability

In the event of a claim, NeroviaFlow’s total liability is limited to the total fees paid by the client in the preceding 12 months. This clause is enforceable under Moroccan law (Dahir 1913, Art. 263). Consequential damages (lost profits, business interruption) are excluded where permitted.

⚖️ Dispute resolution – Moroccan law
Governing law: Moroccan law (Dahir 1913 – Code of Obligations and Contracts).
Jurisdiction: Commercial Court of Casablanca.
Mandatory mediation: Before any litigation, the parties must attempt amicable settlement through a certified mediator (CMCA).
Arbitration clause: Optional for cross‑border disputes (future international contracts).
Statute of limitations: 5 years from the date the cause of action arose.
