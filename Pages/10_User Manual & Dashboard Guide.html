NeroviaFlow – User Manual & Dashboard Guide

For hotel facility managers – Monitor water consumption, detect leaks, generate reports, and manage alerts – Version 1.0
🔍 What is the NeroviaFlow dashboard?

The NeroviaFlow dashboard is your central command for water intelligence. It displays real‑time flow and pressure data, alerts you when a leak is detected, and provides historical reports for ESG compliance and water savings verification. Accessible from any modern web browser (desktop or mobile), the dashboard is designed for quick decision‑making without technical training.

📊 Live monitoring – understanding the charts

Flow rate (L/h)

The blue line shows water flow every 15 minutes. A horizontal dashed line indicates the expected baseline for that time of day (based on 30‑day learning). If the blue line stays above the baseline during night hours (1‑4 AM), the system will investigate a possible leak.

Pressure (bar)

The green line shows water pressure. A sudden drop of more than 0.3 bar, especially when flow is also high, strongly indicates a leak. The dashboard highlights such events in red.

Acoustic energy (if equipped)

The orange line represents vibration/noise on the pipe. When acoustic energy exceeds baseline during night hours, it adds confidence to leak detection.

Baseline learning period

For the first 30 days after installation, the system is in “learning mode”. No alerts will be sent. You can still view data, but the baseline is being established. A progress bar shows remaining days.

⚠️ Alerts – what they mean and how to respond

Alert types

Leak confirmed Red banner: flow anomaly + pressure drop confirmed over 3 consecutive readings. Action: call maintenance, close zone valves to locate leak, then repair.
Potential leak Yellow banner: only one of the conditions (flow or pressure) met. Investigate if repeats.
Sensor offline Gray banner: no data received for >1 hour. Check power or GSM signal.

Alert resolution workflow

  1. Receive SMS and dashboard notification.
  2. Open dashboard, click on alert to see details (time, flow deviation, pressure drop).
  3. Use zone shutoff valves (hotel internal plumbing) to isolate the leak.
  4. After repair, click “Mark as resolved” and enter notes (e.g., “toilet flapper replaced”).
  5. System will automatically verify post‑repair consumption (night flow should return to baseline).

📄 Monthly reports & ESG data

Generating a report

From the left menu, select Reports → Monthly. Choose year and month. Click “Generate PDF”. The report includes:

  • Total water consumption (m³).
  • Estimated water saved (compared to baseline).
  • Leak events and volume lost.
  • CO₂ equivalent avoided (based on energy saved from pumping/treatment).
  • ESG metrics aligned with SDG 6, 11, 13.

ESG dashboard (Premium tier)

Premium subscribers get an ESG‑dedicated view with year‑to‑date trends, benchmarking against similar hotels, and downloadable data for corporate sustainability reports (GRI, SASB formats).

🛠️ Troubleshooting – what to do when

“No data for last 2 hours”

Possible causes: power outage, GSM network down, SIM credit expired. Check the “Device health” panel (shows battery voltage and last transmission). If battery <3.5V, recharge using USB cable. If SIM credit low, contact NeroviaFlow support.

False alert (no leak found)

Click on the alert and select “Mark as false positive”. The system will ask you to describe the event (e.g., “cooling tower cycled”). This helps improve the algorithm. Your feedback is anonymised.

Pressure reading seems wrong

Verify that the pressure sensor valve is fully open. If still incorrect, contact support – a technician may need to recalibrate or replace the sensor.

Cannot log in

Use “Forgot password” link. If that fails, email support@neroviaflow.com with your site ID. Account recovery within 1 business day.

🔒 Security & privacy

All data transmitted over HTTPS. Your raw operational data is never shared with third parties without consent. Anonymised aggregates are used for research and utility partnerships (you can opt out in settings). Two‑factor authentication (2FA) is available for enterprise accounts.
