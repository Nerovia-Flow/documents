🎯 ICP – Ideal Customer Profile (first 24 months)

🏨 Hotel profile

Independent hotels, 50‑150 rooms, built before 2005 (aging infrastructure).
📍 Location: Casablanca (phase 1) → Rabat, Marrakech, Tangier → Southern Europe & MENA.
✅ Must have a mechanical water meter and an accessible pressure test valve (1/4″ BSP).

💸 Economic & pain drivers

Monthly water bill > $1,500 (>15,000 MAD) – waste 20‑40% unnoticed.
❌ No real‑time data – only monthly utility bills.
❌ Maintenance teams react after floods, not before.
✅ ESG pressure from corporate clients & green certifications (LEED, Green Key).

🧑‍💼 Decision‑maker

Facility manager, chief engineer, or owner‑operator.
Speaks Darija/French/English. Motivated by cost reduction, guest comfort, and asset protection.
Trust triggers: Hult Prize win, live dashboard demo, reference from another hotel.

🔑 Willingness to pay

OpEx model: $50 installation + $340‑460/month.
30‑day free trial removes risk. Payback period < 2 months.
After proven savings, hotels readily convert to 3‑5 year contracts.

📦 Marketing Mix – The 4Ps

📌 Product

Non‑invasive clamp‑on ultrasonic flow meter + industrial pressure sensor + battery‑powered GSM gateway. 24/7 monitoring, 30‑day baseline learning, dual‑sensor leak detection (zero false alarms), SMS alerts, live dashboard, monthly reports, optional ESG reporting. Optional acoustic sensor for very small leaks.

💰 Price

Essential: $340/month (leak detection, alerts, dashboard). Premium: $460/month (+ monthly reports, ESG, priority support). Installation: $50 (promotional). 30‑day free trial. No shared savings – client keeps 100% of water saved. Data monetisation: $30/site/month (anonymised aggregates).

📍 Place (Distribution)

Direct B2B sales – founder‑led outreach. Channels: cold calls → site visits → pilots → referrals. Plumber network ($20/lead). No distributors, no online store. Geographic rollout: Casablanca → Morocco → Southern Europe (Spain, Portugal, Italy, Greece) → MENA.

📢 Promotion

Zero marketing budget – proof‑based, relationship‑driven. Free 60‑day trial, referral program ($100 discount), live dashboard demo during site visit. Hult Prize badge, patent pending credibility. LinkedIn thought leadership and case studies after first pilots.
🌍 PESTEL Analysis – Macro‑environment (Morocco focus)

🏛️ Political

National Water Plan 2020‑2027 ($11.5B). Royal Water Efficiency Trophy (2025). Rising tariffs (+40% last decade). Strong government push for water efficiency – tailwind for adoption.

📈 Economic

Water tariffs up +8.5% in 2024 (LYDEC). Tourism recovery → higher hotel water bills. IoT sensor prices collapsed (clamp‑on ultrasonic now $350 bulk). OpEx subscription fits hotel budgets. Capital‑efficient bootstrapping.

👥 Sociocultural

Morocco lost 75% of water per capita in one generation – high awareness. ESG pressure on hotels (corporate reporting, guest expectations). Maintenance culture reactive, but automated alerts shift behaviour.

💻 Technological

Clamp‑on ultrasonic mature, industrial pressure sensors cheap, GSM ubiquitous, cloud platforms free. No R&D risk. Patent pending (OMPIC) – dual‑sensor + non‑invasive clamp‑on.

⚖️ Legal

Law 09‑08 (data protection): client owns raw data; NeroviaFlow uses only anonymised aggregates. Law 36‑15 (water quality): sensors are SS316L/brass with food‑grade PTFE. No legal barrier. Patent filing secures IP.

🌱 Environmental

Water stress: 606 m³/person/year → <500 m³ by 2030 (absolute scarcity). Climate change → droughts, need for efficiency. Saving water saves energy (pumping/treatment) → reduces CO₂ (SDG 13).

⚔️ Porter’s Five Forces – Industry structure
1. Threat of new entrants
Medium‑High. Hardware is off‑the‑shelf, but data moat (30‑day baseline per building) and first‑mover contracts create high barriers. Copycats would lack historical data and trust.
2. Buyer power
High initially, but free trial + low entry price remove risk. Once contracted, switching costs high (loss of data + dashboard). Power decreases after proven savings.
3. Supplier power
Low. Multiple sources for flow meters (Sino‑Inst, LORRIC), pressure sensors (Cynergy3, TE, Balluff), ESP32, GSM modules. No single supplier is irreplaceable.
4. Threat of substitutes
Medium. “Do nothing” (pay high bills) is free upfront but costly. Plumber inspections are reactive. Consumer devices (Bluebot) are flow‑only → false alarms. NeroviaFlow’s dual‑sensor + GSM is superior.
5. Rivalry among existing competitors
Low. No direct competitor targets 80‑150 room hotels with non‑invasive flow+pressure+GSM+baseline learning. Bluebot is consumer/SMB; industrial BMS too expensive.
