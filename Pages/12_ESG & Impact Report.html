📌 Executive summary – Why impact matters

NeroviaFlow directly addresses one of the world's most pressing environmental crises: water waste in buildings. By detecting hidden leaks, we prevent the loss of treated water, reduce energy consumption for pumping and treatment, and lower greenhouse gas emissions. Our solution is designed to scale, delivering measurable ESG outcomes for hotel clients and contributing to Morocco's water security targets.

📊 Methodology – How we measure impact

Water saved (litres)

Baseline 30‑day average night flow (1‑4 AM) before leak repair.
Post‑repair Average night flow after leak is fixed.
Annual savings (Baseline − Post‑repair) × 365 days.
Assumption 20‑40% waste typical; we use conservative 20% reduction per site = 240,000 litres/site/year.

CO₂ equivalent (tCO₂e)

Based on International Energy Agency (IEA) water‑energy nexus data:
• 0.6 kg CO₂ per m³ for water supply and treatment (pumping, filtration).
• Additional 0.2 kg CO₂ per m³ for heating (hotels with water heaters, conservative).
Weighted average used: 0.8 kg CO₂/m³ (conservative).

👥 Social impact & vulnerable groups

Direct social benefits

• Hotels reduce operational costs, improving financial resilience.
• Water savings in water‑stressed regions (e.g., Morocco) benefit all residents, including low‑income communities.
• Job creation targets youth and technicians in Casablanca (unemployment rate ~15%).
• Salaries and social charges contribute to local economy (~$1.2M/year by Y10).

Plumber referral network

We partner with local plumbers, paying $20 per lead. This generates income for small businesses and informal workers, contributing to local economic development. Estimated 200+ indirect jobs by Y10.

🇲🇦 Alignment with Morocco's water & climate strategy

National Water Plan 2020‑2027

Targets 20% reduction in water losses by 2027. NeroviaFlow directly contributes by providing real‑time leak detection for buildings, which are a major source of NRW.

Morocco's NDC (Nationally Determined Contribution)

Commitment to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by 45.5% by 2030. Water efficiency reduces energy demand, supporting this goal.
