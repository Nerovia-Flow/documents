🔬 Sensor specifications

💧 Clamp‑on ultrasonic flow meter

Measurement Volume flow rate (L/h, m³/h)
Accuracy ±1‑2% of reading
Pipe size DN15 – DN50 (½″ – 2″)
Output 4‑20 mA (2‑wire) or pulse
Power 10‑24 VDC (powered by ESP32 5V via conditioning)
Mounting Clamp‑on, no pipe cutting
Media Clean water, no particles required

📊 Industrial pressure sensor

Range 0 – 10 bar (gauge)
Output 4‑20 mA (2‑wire)
Accuracy ±0.25% full scale
Connection 1/4″ BSP male thread
Wetted parts Stainless steel 316L
Power 10‑30 VDC (powered by ESP32 5V via boost? Actually 4‑20mA loop powered, we use 3.3V reference with 250Ω resistor – works)

🎙️ Acoustic sensor (optional)

Type Piezoelectric contact microphone
Frequency response 20 Hz – 20 kHz
Output 0‑5V analog (amplified)
Mounting Epoxy or magnetic clamp on pipe surface
Power 5V @ 5mA

🔋 Battery & power management

Cell 18650 Li‑ion (2600 mAh)
Nominal voltage 3.7V
Charge circuit TP4056 (micro‑USB, 1A)
Boost converter MT3608 (to 5V for GSM module) – not always needed; SIM800L can run directly from Li‑ion (3.7‑4.2V).
Deep sleep current ESP32 ~6 µA, SIM800L ~1 mA (powered down), sensors off (MOSFET).
Active current ESP32 ~80 mA, sensors ~10 mA, GSM transmit ~200 mA (peaks 2A).

🧠 Microcontroller & communication

ESP32 WROOM‑32

CPU Dual‑core Xtensa LX6 @ 240 MHz
RAM 520 KB SRAM
Flash 4 MB (external)
ADC 12‑bit, 18 channels (used for pressure, acoustic)
Digital I/O Used for Hall sensor pulse counting, MOSFET control, GSM power key.
Deep sleep Timer wake‑up (15 min).
Programming Arduino / PlatformIO.

SIM800L GSM module

Network 2G (GSM/GPRS) – compatible with Maroc Telecom & Inwi.
Data rate Up to 85.6 kbps (GPRS class 12).
Power supply 3.7‑4.2V (direct from Li‑ion battery).
Power key Controlled by ESP32 GPIO to turn on/off.
Antenna SMA connector, external antenna for pit installations.
AT commands Used for HTTP POST and SMS.

Power distribution

  • 18650 Li‑ion → ESP32 VIN (through protection diode)
  • 18650 Li‑ion → SIM800L VCC (direct, with 1000µF capacitor across VCC/GND)
  • 18650 Li‑ion → TP4056 (charging)
  • ESP32 3.3V → Hall sensor (via MOSFET)
  • ESP32 3.3V → acoustic sensor (via MOSFET)
  • MT3608 boost (optional) 3.7V→5V → pressure sensor (4‑20mA loop powered from 5V + 250Ω resistor to ground).

Pit installation considerations

• Desiccant packs inside to prevent condensation.
• Silicone sealant around lid edge.
• Drip loops on external cables.
• External antenna (magnetic base) for GSM if signal weak.
• Hose clamp + L‑bracket for Hall sensor (if meter used).

🛠️ Installation procedure (hardware)
  1. Site survey – Verify mechanical water meter, accessible pressure test valve, GSM signal, and space for enclosure.
  2. Mount flow meter – Clean pipe surface, apply acoustic gel, clamp transducers, secure with stainless steel strap.
  3. Install pressure sensor – Wrap PTFE tape, screw into test valve hand‑tight + ¼ turn.
  4. Optional acoustic sensor – Attach to pipe surface with epoxy or magnetic clamp.
  5. Prepare enclosure – Drill holes for cable glands, mount ESP32, SIM800L, battery, TP4056, and PCB inside.
  6. Wire connections – Follow wiring diagram, double‑check polarity, add 1000µF capacitor across SIM800L VCC/GND.
  7. Insert SIM card – Maroc Telecom or Inwi, data plan activated.
  8. Power up – Connect battery, USB charger (optional), verify LED status.
  9. Test GSM – Send test SMS via serial command.
  10. Close enclosure – Tighten glands, add desiccant, seal lid.
  11. Mount enclosure – On wall or pit side, secure with screws.
  12. Verify data – Wait 15 minutes, check cloud dashboard for first reading.
🔁 Reliability, maintenance & lifecycle

Expected lifetime

• Flow meter: 5‑10 years (no moving parts).
• Pressure sensor: 5‑10 years (stainless steel diaphragm).
• ESP32 / GSM: 5‑10 years (solid‑state).
• Battery: 2‑3 years (rechargeable, 500 cycles).
• Enclosure & glands: 10+ years.

Maintenance schedule

• Monthly: Check battery voltage via dashboard.
• Quarterly: Inspect enclosure seals, cable glands, sensor attachments.
• Annually: Replace desiccant, test GSM signal.
• Battery replacement every 2‑3 years (user‑replaceable via USB charging).
